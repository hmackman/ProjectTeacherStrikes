Creating a bridge between educators and the public is crucial. If you have a child in school, ask their teacher about their experiences. Or reach out to a teacher that made an impact on your life. There are a number of great resources to learn more. Here are a few that we recommend:

News Article - USA Today:
  • "We followed 15 of America's teachers on a day of frustrations, pressures and hard-earned victories"by USA TODAY Network
    • Podcasts/Radio Vignettes- National Public Radio (NPR):
  • "School Money:The Cost of Opportunity"
    • U.S. Department of Education - Data and Statistics:
  • The federal agency charged with public school oversight regularly publishes data on a variety of subjects and allows you to download excel files.

    Connect with educators about public school funding through social media. Attend a local school board meeting, vote for local and state millages that would increase school funding, and/or get involved with local organizations that promote public schools.

    3. Donate School Supplies

    There are many platforms that allow individuals, corporations, or other groups to help teachers buy school supplies or give children opportunities. One of the these Organizations is DonorsChoose. This organization was started by a former public school teacher in the New York City.
